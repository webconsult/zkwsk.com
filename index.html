Projects

I have been working for large companies as well as early-stage startups and I see different opportunities in either. Below are a handful of the companies and organizations I have been working for in the past...

Front-end developer

During spring 2014 I was working as a front-end developer at AIRTAME.

AIRTAME had recently managed to close the - at that time - biggest crowdfunding round in Scandinavia, at $1.3 million and was in great need of talented developers.

I was hired to do front-end development on their main website, but eventually ended up spending most of my time developing an internal system for managing the contributions from the many supporters of the project.

When time allowed for it I also made smaller contributions to the main website.

Unfortunately I cannot show a live preview of the application I built, but you are welcome to get in touch for more info.

Front-end & UX Designer

I was part of bootstrapping the startup Fourpio - a webapp that provides tools for four people to collaborate on ideas and opinions.

Even though we where aiming for the sky and the founder, Tim Allison, already had several startups and an exit in his luggage, we ended up choosing to put the project aside in favor of other opportunities.

We where working out of Founders House, an ambitious co-working space in Copenhagen, and while my responsibility was front-end design, I was highly involved in both UX and adapting the business model.

Front-end & UX Designer

During summer 2013 I was part of a team of developers and designers who got to travel to Mauritius on a developer bootcamp. The aim was to build a music app for the danish recording company Tigerspring Records.

In the morning we trained karate, daytimes where spend designing and coding and evenings where spend at local restaurants and bars. The weekends where reserved for going out and sightseeing.

I got to hone my skills in front-end development as well as UX design. During the first few weeks I took part in conducting user-research, building personas and scenarios. Then we did the information architecture and build wireframes and finally I was responsible for implementing the designs using Twitter Bootstrap on top of .NET-based application stack.

The actual product is protected by an NDA - and has still not been launched - but above is a prototype I built for Tigerspring before the trip which is excluded from the NDA.
